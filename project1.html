Premier League mobile app redesign

Modernizing the design of the Premier League application to bring the football fans back to the "official" application

Overview

Despite the popularity of its game, the Premier League struggles to attract and engage with its millions of football fans on its official application platform. Driven by my own curiosity, I redesigned the Premier League’s mobile application. This personal project demonstrates my capabiliites in user research, usability testing via design heuristics, and high-fidelity prototyping. My goal was to design for a modern and highly usable app that leads to higher user adoption.

Project Type

Personal project

My Role

  • User researcher
  • Product Designer

01. Problem Space

The struggle with low usage.

I’m a fan of the English Premier League, a star-studded football league in England. However, I don’t have the official app on my phone. To my surprise, I found out that many of my friends who also love the league did not own the app nor use it.

The official Premier League app is the face of the premiership football with a huge potential for its millions of football fans. Yet, a quick online search suggested a host of other sports news platforms with reviews that clearly preferred third-party apps.

Why is user adoption for the official mobile application so low compared to other websites?

Why does this matter?

In my honest opinion, it’s a dire situation, especially as betting companies like DraftKings continue to poach users from the official platform. It’s bad news for Premier League that monetizes not only from broadcasting rights but also from ads sponsors right on its website and mobile application.

I believe that user retention / adoption should be a high-priority business objective for the Premier League

02. Research

Getting the facts straight

To quickly validate my assumption that the Premier League is scarecely used, I deployed a web survey and conducted interviews with several football fans. The 2 questions I was the most interested in were -

  • What information do users expect from a sports fan?
  • How are they accessing this information today, if not on the offical Premier League app?

Survey results and observations

Results indicated that only 3 individuals out of the 21 actually had the app and used it. I also found out that the primary user needs were fairly consistent across, with match-related information (e.g., live scores, upcoming fixtures) being the most important features they seek for.

During the user interviews, I noted down critical user needs and their existing pain points on various sports apps, including the offical Premier League app, many of which touched upon the design aspect.

User needs against the existing features

To further make sense of my research, I used affinity diagram to group and label all of the observed user needs. I also overlayed it with indication of whether that information or feature set was available on the official Premier League app. Not surprisingly, most of the information were readily available in the current version of the app.

Research insights and synthesis

I validated my assumption that the Premier League app is not widely used by football fans. More importantly, I learned that users not only want to see all the relevant information but also expect sports app to present them in good design. 3 conclusions were derived from my research phase:

  • The official Premier League app is struggling with low user adoption and low usage (N=21)
  • The driving factor for this issue is not the lack of information or features available in the current version of the app.
  • Presenting information in an easily scannable and digestible way is critical for user experience, especially in the context of sports

With these insights in mind, I re-framed the initial problem space into a more actionable design problem statement

How might we present information on the Premier League app so that users can consume them with more ease?

03. App Critique

Taking a closer look

To better assess potential issues and evaluate how well the current design aligns with the identified user needs, I conducted an app critique using Nielsen Norman’s usability heuristics.

Usability heuristics

While the app design was modern and edgy, several usability-related challenges were evident across the various tabs in the app. Two usability heuristics stood out to be the challenge.

Takeaways for re-design

The official Premier League application had information, perhaps too much in a disorganized way. Tying this back to the user preference of easily consusmable information, it seemed like many of the usability issues stemmed from an ineffective information architecture.

04. Design

Design directions

With research and app critique completed, I approached the re-design of the Premier League app with three design principles in mind.

Screen wireframes

I started out by developing wireframes to demonstrate the type of interface elements I would like to place on key pages including the Latest, Standings, Fantasy Football, and Your Club pages.

Final design

After several design iterations while being grounded on the three design directions, I completed the redesign for the official Premier League mobile app.

01. HOME PAGE

Where the live action is

On the very first page of the app, users will find all they need to know about what is happening right now.

[Pain point addressed]

As the one page that is dedicated to only the information that is truly happening now, all other information that previously cluttered the view are effectively removed

02. LATEST NEWS

Tell me what’s new

The News tab is home to all club, player, and league news. Users can filter content based on topics using the top row filters

[Pain point addressed]

Clear tab labels prevent potential misguided navigation to news content, one of the most important feature in a sports app, Users can also filter page to view specific topics.

03. YOUR CLUB

It’s all about my team

At the center of the app is the user’s home club, displaying match schedule, up-to-date league standing, and key players stats.

[Pain point addressed]

Accessing data on user's home club required searching for individual bits of info on multiple pages. A single, dedicated home club page increases findability.

05. Reflection

Learning moments

This project was born out of a simple question but led to avenues that were much bigger:

  • How does the Premier League make money?
  • Why are some platforms favorited by more football fans?
  • What design changes can better position Premier League and attract more users?

Measuring impact

Micro impact - The new design was focused on improving usability and therefore measuring usability metrics will be a key factor to determine the success of my redesign. In particular, I would prioritize measuring 'time to task' to assess findability and navigation.

Macro impact - As noted in the initial problem space, the ultimate goal of my redesign is to ramp up usage and grow users on the official platform. I would be interested in measuring the effect of the new design on the overall user adoption and retention.
